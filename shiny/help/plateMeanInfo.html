This plot depicts the mean signal of each plate in the screen. This gives an impression of variation effects such as

  • Time effects: In a cell based RNAi screen, for instance, transfection efficiency decreases the longer the cells are in suspension. This means that plates seeded later (usually indicated by a high plate number) show a lower average signal.
  • Replicate performance: Replicate plates should obviously have the same average signal. Depending on how the replicate plates have been created, this might not always be the case.
  • Library bias: If a screening library is not randomized, the plate average might vary significantly.

All these factors have to be taken into account when chosing an appropriate normalization strategy.
